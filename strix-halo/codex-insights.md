**Bottom Line**
As of April 18, 2026, the recent commits in this fork are mostly research notes, not missed code changes: `cce970166` (baseline), `3b8fea808` (K/V isolation), `309b410e2` (FA dispatcher dead end), and `01481a1ec` (UMA note). After reading the repository [README.md](../README.md), [kv-cache.md](kv-cache.md), [fa-dispatcher.md](fa-dispatcher.md), [uma-integrated.md](uma-integrated.md), [qwen3.6-baseline.md](qwen3.6-baseline.md), and [NOTES.md](NOTES.md), the fork’s conclusions mostly line up with the public story: the biggest PP problem on gfx1151 is not UMA/“integrated GPU” handling, it’s missing or weak gfx1151-specific kernel tuning, incomplete flash-attention coverage, and MoE-specific bottlenecks.

**What Looks Real**

- The strongest immediate PP lever is still KV policy. Your local numbers are dramatic: on Qwen3.6 35B-A3B, `pp512 @16k` is about `736 t/s` with `f16/f16` KV and about `45 t/s` with `f16/q4_0`. For prompt processing, quantized `V` looks like the wrong tradeoff on the current gfx1151 HIP/TILE FA path.
- The highest-confidence code opportunity is gfx1151-specific MMQ/MMVQ tuning. Your [NOTES.md](NOTES.md) suspicion that RDNA3.5 is inheriting bad defaults is strongly supported by [llama.cpp issue #21284](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/21284) and [PR #21344](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/21344). Independent Strix Halo retests on April 3, 2026 showed Qwen3.5-122B going from `343 -> 454 t/s` at `pp512` and `455 -> 530 t/s` at `pp4096`, with much larger gains at smaller PP sizes.
- Your FA dispatcher writeup also matches upstream discussion. Widening a dispatch gate is not enough if the MMA_F16 device code/templates are not actually built for gfx1151. That makes “real gfx1151 MMA flash-attention support” a plausible next major win, but it is a deeper project than MMQ tuning.
- MoE is a separate hotspot. [Issue #17014](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/17014) tied a 15-20% HIP PP regression on Strix Halo to a specific MoE kernel change, and [issue #21948](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/issues/21948) says Vulkan `MUL_MAT_ID` dominates large-MoE prefill on gfx1151. If your main targets are Qwen/GPT-OSS MoE models, that path deserves focused attention after MMQ/MMVQ.
- The local decision to de-prioritize UMA/integrated tuning looks correct. It matches [PR #16308](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/16308), where the analogous integrated-GPU host-buffer change did not move perf meaningfully.
- The local choice to prefer TheRock-style ROCm stacks also looks justified. [ROCm issue #6042](https://github.com/ROCm/ROCm/issues/6042) from March 18, 2026 ended with AMD recommending a clean TheRock/runtime pairing to avoid invalid kernel image failures on gfx1151.

**The Broader gfx1151 Story**

- A lot of contradictory advice comes from the stack changing underneath people. [ROCm issue #4748](https://github.com/ROCm/ROCm/issues/4748) shows native gfx1151 rocBLAS was historically much worse than gfx1100/hipBLASLt paths, which is why older threads recommend hacks like gfx-version overrides or hipBLASLt env vars.
- Community sites still paint ROCm as the best PP backend when it is tuned, while Vulkan often holds up better for TG or very deep context: [Strix Halo Wiki ROCm guide](https://strixhalo.wiki/AI/llamacpp-with-ROCm), [their performance page](https://strixhalo.wiki/AI/llamacpp-performance), and [kyuz0’s benchmark grid](https://kyuz0.github.io/amd-strix-halo-toolboxes/).
- Older community guidance leaned on `rocWMMA + hipBLASLt`, but that advice aged. The same wiki now notes that, on newer ROCm/upstream llama.cpp, upstream `rocWMMA` gets worse at depth unless you use a custom tuned branch, which matches the tuned-branch discussion in this [LocalLLaMA thread](https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/comments/1ok7hd4/faster_llamacpp_rocm_performance_for_amd_rdna3/).
- Stability is still part of the picture, not just speed. [ROCm issue #5151](https://github.com/ROCm/ROCm/issues/5151) tracks hangs/reset behavior on gfx1151, so some “performance” folklore is really people converging on the fastest setup that also stays alive.

If I were picking the next experiments for this fork, I’d do them in this order: port the [#21344](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp/pull/21344) gfx1151 MMQ/MMVQ changes, keep KV at `f16/f16` for PP benchmarking, audit the neighboring RDNA3.5 gates already called out in [NOTES.md](NOTES.md), and only then consider a real gfx1151 MMA flash-attention/codegen effort.
